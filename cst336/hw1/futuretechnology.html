What's in store for Computer Graphics?

Computer Graphics, although being prominent in our everyday life is still a growing field. The advancement of real-time rendering, GPU technology, and AI have rapidly increased productivity and allows developers to create visualizations faster than before. The future of this technology may reach new potentials with the next step being Real-Time photorealism, Quantum or Cloud rendering, and advancement in AI-generated materials. It's likely to focus on concepts such as immersion with augmented reality with large-scale visuals and realistic motion capture. While the advancements for these technologies are great they do raise some ethical questions presumably in artistic ownership, authenticity of mediums, and scalability along with the environmental impacts.

Here are some examples of the technologies currently being researched:

Technology Description Impact
AI-Assisted Rendering Utilization of neural networks allowing for upscale and predictive visual details. Improves and reduces rendering times and hardware costs.
Generative Graphics Combining AI and 3D models to generate scenes and textures seamlessly. Revolutionizing content creation in films, gaming, and production.
Humanoid Robotics Utilizating Nvidia NUM microservices to develop simulations in order to train humanoid robots. Increase Productivity and reducing human demonstration data inputs.

Articles on the Future of Computer Graphics from Siggraph

Making Scientific Discoveries Accessible to Everyone

Specialists at NASA are working to make visual scientific data more engaging with general population. By doing so they are converting raw sattelite-model data into visualizations using graphical tools such as Houdini, Maya and custom graphical piplining. The underlying problem is that the visualizations are large-scaled reaching issues with computational power and decisions for how much data can be shown.

Generative AI in Computer Graphics

An opinionated article addressing the changes in computer graphics workflow. Discusses the opportunities and challenges many industry studios face. The article tackles the rise of generative AI noting that it allows artists to experiment with ideas much quicker than in the past. It mentions the computational problems and randomness of generated content, mentioning that artists are looking for consistent feedback. Raises the question of how AI can maintain scalability, consistency and collaborative workflows.

Will AI Completely Take Over Computer Graphics?

The article tackles the question on whether AI will completely transform and take over the Computer Graphics field. It discusses the overall adoption of AI within computer graphics pipelines and how it is taking over ever aspect from hardware processing, APIs, application and Tools. Briefly discussing the investment in AI for future graphics systems. It further mentions the ethical concerns of AI on job security to the decrease of human involvement in production.
